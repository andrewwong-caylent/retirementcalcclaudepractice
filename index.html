About You

How many years will your savings need to last? Used to calculate monthly withdrawals.

Canada Pension Plan (CPP)

CPP is a government pension based on your contributions while working. The maximum monthly amount in 2024 is ~$1,364.

Starting earlier means smaller monthly payments. Starting later means larger ones.

Old Age Security (OAS)

OAS is a flat monthly benefit paid to most Canadians at age 65. The maximum in 2024 is ~$713/month.

You need 40 years for the full amount. Fewer years = proportionally less.

Personal Savings

Enter your current balance and monthly contribution for each account type you have. Leave at 0 for accounts you don't use.

RRSP

Registered Retirement Savings Plan. Contributions are tax-deductible; withdrawals are taxed as income.

TFSA

Tax-Free Savings Account. Contributions are not tax-deductible, but growth and withdrawals are tax-free.

Non-Registered

Regular investment account. No contribution limits, but investment gains are taxable each year.

Cash Savings

Savings account, GICs, or cash. Lower risk and lower return than investments.

Inflation Adjustment

Inflation means money loses purchasing power over time. $1,000/month today won't feel like $1,000/month in 30 years.

The Bank of Canada targets ~2% inflation. 2-3% is a reasonable assumption.
When on, amounts are adjusted so you can compare them to your income today.
